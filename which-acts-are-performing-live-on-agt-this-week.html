America’s Got Talent Season 18 returns on Tuesday night with the third live show of the season, as 11 more acts take the stage for America’s vote. Only two acts will make it to the finale during Wednesday’s results show.

AGT has already shared which acts will be performing on this week’s episode, airing on NBC. Once again, this week’s show will feature two Golden Buzzer acts. Read on to find out who this week’s acts are and revisit their impressive auditions from earlier in the season.

Who’s Performing on AGT Tonight?

Barry Brewer Jr.

This comedian auditioned for Season 18 on crutches due to a sports injury. He made the judges laugh as he played the keyboard and told jokes about his experiences performing in church. The panel chose to send Barry to the live shows.

Herwan Legaillard

This French performer took the stage in the Season 18 auditions and stunned the judges with his stylish approach to sword-swallowing. He earned a standing ovation from the panel, who sent him to the live shows for a chance at America’s vote.

Justin Jackson

This talented performer delivered an energetic tap dance routine during his audition earlier this season, making the judges smile and dance along. They chose to send him to the live shows, where he’ll compete for his chance to appear in the finale.

MOS

This all-female brass band from Japan brought the funk to the AGT stage in auditions with their rendition of “Bang Bang.” The crowd went wild for them, and the panel chose to send them to the Season 18 live shows for a shot at America’s vote.

Mzansi Youth Choir

This choir from South Africa delivered a powerful performance of “It’s OK” by late AGT contestant Nightbirde. They ended up earning the show’s first-ever audience-chosen Golden Buzzer, sending them straight to the live shows.

Philip Bowen

This violinist wowed the judges with his instrumental performance of “Chop Suey!” by System of a Down. The panel was so impressed, they chose to send Philip to the Season 18 live shows for a chance to appear in the finale.

Phil Wright & Parent Jam

This fun-loving dance group gives parents and their kids the opportunity to take the stage together. Their audition made the judges smile and earned them a spot in the live shows. Now they’re competing for a spot in the Season 18 Finals.

Putri Ariani

This 17-year-old blind singer from Indonesia previously won Indonesia’s Got Talent at 8 years old. For her audition, she played piano and sang an original song called “Loneliness.” Judge Simon Cowell chose to hit the Golden Buzzer for her, sending her straight to the live shows.

Summer Rios

This 19-year-old singer battled nerves to perform “Something in the Orange” by Zach Bryan in her audition. The performance impressed the judges enough to give her a spot in the Season 18 live shows. Now she’s competing for a spot in the finale.

Trigg Watson

This performer decided to combine technology and magic for his unique act. His audition involved interacting with a giant phone screen. The panel liked it enough to send Trigg through to the live shows for a chance at America’s vote.

Warrior Squad

This acrobatic dance group from India wowed the audience and judges with their high-flying audition, earning a standing ovation and a spot in the live shows. Now they’re competing for a chance to go to the Finals.
