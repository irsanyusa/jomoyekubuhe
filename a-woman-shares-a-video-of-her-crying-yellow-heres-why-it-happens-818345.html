A Woman Shares a Video of Her Crying Yellow: Heres Why It Happens

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, September 15, 2024

When tears turn yellow, it can happen due to various reasons, but it’s important to note that this doesn’t necessarily indicate a life-threatening situation. However, in a popular TikTok video, someone filmed themselves crying and noticed that their tears were yellow. This has sparked curiosity among viewers, who are now wondering why tears can appear yellow. Here’s what we know about this phenomenon and what might be causing it.

If you’re experiencing emotional distress or simply feel the need to cry, the color of your tears is probably the least of your concerns.

Typically, tears appear clear or translucent, resembling plain water for most people. People can cry yellow tears due to various reasons, depending on the specific appearance of their tears. The color of tears is typically influenced by their composition, which includes water, oil, and mucus. If tears appear significantly more yellow than usual, it may be due to an excess of oil in the tear ducts.

The meibomian glands in your eyes produce oil to lubricate them and prevent dryness. When these glands produce an excessive amount of oil, it can mix with tears, causing them to appear yellow and feel greasy.

Yellow tears can also result from other factors, such as infections or allergies. In these cases, the tears may be yellow due to pus from an infection or as a reaction to external irritants.

If you’ve observed any additional symptoms along with your yellow tears, it’s advisable to seek medical attention.

Monitor your tears closely, and if the yellow color persists for several days, consult a healthcare professional. While yellow tears are unlikely to be life-threatening, they are not considered normal and should be addressed.

While yellow tears may have gained attention on social media, tears can actually come in various colors. For instance, tears can be red or pink if there’s bleeding in or around the eye, and they can even be black. Black deposits can be caused by prolonged exposure to silver or shedding from the subconjunctival mycetoma, among other possible things, and these can be quite serious and should not be taken lightly.

Generally, tear production is a healthy aspect of bodily function, even if the color seems unusual.

However, it’s still a good idea to keep an eye on any changes in your tear color and seek medical advice if needed.

The viral video on TikTok about yellow tears has sparked concern among viewers. Comments on the video range from humorous to slightly anxious reactions, with people expressing their thoughts on the unusual phenomenon. The woman actually shared that the yellow color from this specific video is just from her eye appointment according to her caption— which could be fluorescein dye, a diagnostic contrast agent particularly used in various ophthalmic procedures, such as checking for any corneal or vessel abnormalities.

Yellow tears, while uncommon, are often a harmless and temporary occurrence caused by various factors like excess oil or reactions to irritants. Understanding that tears can come in different colors is fascinating, and it’s a reminder of the complexity of our bodies.
